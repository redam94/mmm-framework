Documented Assumptions

identification causal_identification
Value: "All four media channels (TV, Digital, Paid_Social, Radio) are causally identified via backdoor adjustment on {Price_Index, Distribution, Seasonality}"
Rationale: Pearl's backdoor criterion is satisfied for all four treatment→Sales paths. The only open backdoor paths run through Seasonality, which is blocked by conditioning on seasonal components in the model. Price_Index and Distribution are included to block Economy-related paths and supply-side variation respectively.
causal_structure dag_structure
Value: {"nodes": [{"id": "sales", "variable_name": "Sales", "node_type": "kpi", "label": null, "dimensions": ["Period"], "config": {}}, {"id": "tv", "variable_name"…
Rationale: TV, Digital, Paid Social, and Radio each have a direct causal effect on Sales through awareness and demand generation. Price_Index and Distribution are control variables that independently shift Sales — Distribution affects how available the product is (supply-side), while Price_Index affects consumer willingness to buy (demand-side). Seasonality is an unmeasured confounder that simultaneously drives media investment decisions (brands spend more in peak seasons) and consumer purchasing behaviour, creating a backdoor path from media to Sales that must be blocked by conditioning on seasonal controls. Economy is a confounder that affects both Price_Index (input costs / competitive pricing) and Sales (consumer spending power). No mediators are included at this stage — all media effects are assumed to be direct (or via fast-acting mechanisms not separately measured).
functional_form digital_adstock
Value: "Geometric adstock, l_max=4 weeks"
Rationale: Digital is a performance channel with fast-acting, short-lived effects. Carryover beyond 4 weeks is unlikely for paid search/display. l_max=4 is sufficient.
causal_structure economy_confounder
Value: "Economy affects both Sales directly; Price_Index is treated as an observed control (not downstream of Economy in the DAG)"
Rationale: The DAG tool does not support confounder-to-control edges. Economy's effect on Price_Index is implicitly captured by including Price_Index as a control variable — conditioning on Price_Index blocks the Economy→Price_Index→Sales path.

other inference_settings
Value: "4 chains x 2000 draws, tune=1000, target_accept=0.85"
Rationale: 2000 posterior draws per chain (8000 total) provides sufficient ESS for stable credible intervals. 4 chains enables R-hat convergence diagnostics. target_accept=0.85 balances sampling efficiency with divergence avoidance.
functional_form no_weekly_seasonality
Value: "Weekly seasonality set to 0 (disabled)"
Rationale: Data is at weekly granularity — there is no within-week variation to model. Weekly Fourier terms would be meaningless and risk absorbing media signal into the seasonal component, biasing ROI estimates. Yearly seasonality (4 Fourier terms) is sufficient to capture all meaningful seasonal patterns.
functional_form paid_social_adstock
Value: "Geometric adstock, l_max=3 weeks"
Rationale: Paid Social sits between TV and Digital in terms of effect duration. Social ads have short attention windows; l_max=3 weeks captures the likely decay window.
prior prior_predictive_negative_draws
Value: "Prior predictive check reports 8.7% negative draws on normalised scale; accepted as non-problematic"
Rationale: The prior predictive check operates on z-scored (normalised) Sales. A "negative" draw on the normalised scale simply means Sales below the mean (£1.3M), NOT truly negative Sales. Since actual Sales range from £853K to £1.74M, values below the mean are entirely plausible (51% of observed weeks are below the mean). The 5th–95th percentile prior range [£1.3M–£2.4M] is somewhat tight on the upside but not implausible. Proceeding to full fit.
functional_form radio_adstock
Value: "Geometric adstock, l_max=4 weeks"
Rationale: Radio is a reach/frequency channel often used alongside TV. Effects are shorter than TV but longer than pure digital. l_max=4 is a reasonable upper bound.
functional_form saturation_all_channels
Value: "Hill saturation function applied to all four media channels"
Rationale: The Hill function is a flexible, industry-standard saturation curve with two parameters (slope and half-saturation point). It captures diminishing returns for all channels — doubling spend does not double sales. Applied universally as no channel-specific evidence suggests a different functional form.
functional_form seasonality
Value: "Yearly seasonality with 4 Fourier terms; weekly seasonality with 2 Fourier terms"
Rationale: Yearly seasonality (4 Fourier pairs) captures annual demand cycles such as Q4 peaks, summer troughs, and promotional periods. This is the primary backdoor blocker for the Seasonality confounder identified in the DAG. Weekly seasonality (2 Fourier pairs) captures within-week variation in consumer behaviour. 4 yearly terms is sufficient for smooth annual patterns without overfitting over 5 years of data.
functional_form trend
Value: "Piecewise linear trend with 10 changepoints"
Rationale: A piecewise linear trend allows the baseline growth rate to shift at up to 10 points over 5 years (roughly every 6 months), capturing structural changes in organic demand without overfitting. This is critical for correctly attributing sales to media vs. underlying brand growth.
functional_form tv_adstock
Value: "Geometric adstock, l_max=8 weeks"
Rationale: TV is a brand-building channel with long carryover. Industry evidence suggests TV effects can persist 6–10 weeks. l_max=8 allows the model to learn decay rates up to 8 weeks.
