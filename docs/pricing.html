Pricing

The MMM Framework is open-core. The full modeling library and the platform are free and open source under Apache 2.0 — you can self-host the whole thing, with no seat, project, or fit limits, forever. The paid tiers below are for the managed multi-tenant posture: a hosted workspace where the differentiators are exactly the multi-tenancy, SSO, sandboxed-execution, and branding capabilities a single-tenant self-host doesn't need.

Dollar prices on this page are proposed and illustrative — final pricing is TBD. The seat / project / monthly-fit limits and the feature gates are the real, enforceable contract (they come straight from src/mmm_framework/auth/plans.py). The dollar figures are a starting point the founder will set; treat them as a sketch, not a quote.

Open core: free and unlimited when self-hosted

The "open" in open-core is not a billed plan

Plan limits only apply in hosted multi-tenant mode (auth enabled). When you self-host the OSS core with authentication off — the default install — it runs as a single-tenant dev principal with no seat, project, or fit limits at all (auth/plans.py lines 51–53). That is a separate thing from the Free billed plan below: the Free plan is the entry point on the managed platform; self-hosting is the unlimited open-source path.

So the decision is simple: run it yourself for free with no limits, or let us run the multi-tenant platform for you and pay for the managed capabilities and the compute. Everything below is about the second path.

Feature flags are the six in auth/plans.py:FEATURES — multi_tenant, hosted_sandbox, branding, sso, audit_export, priority_support. Free has none; Team adds the first three; Business adds the last three; Enterprise has all six plus a custom SLA / on-prem deployment.

How billing works

Billing is per-seat plus metered fits. Each org is metered against its plan in three dimensions — seats (org members), projects, and fits this month (rows in the run_metrics table since the first of the month) — and you can see your live usage versus your limits any time at GET /auth/usage (auth/plans.py:org_usage). The seat cap is enforced at invite-accept, the project cap at project create, and premium features return HTTP 402 if your plan doesn't include them.

What a "fit" is, and why fits cost money

A fit is one Bayesian model run. A national NUTS fit completes in ~15 seconds, and the NumPyro sampler runs ~3× faster than stock PyMC at equal draws — so a single national model is cheap. But extension models (nested mediation, multivariate, combined) and larger geo / geo×product panels do more work per fit and cost more, which is why fits are metered rather than flat-rated. The monthly fit quota on each tier is the metered envelope; Enterprise removes the cap.

What drives the price (COGS)

The platform is open source and self-hosted by default, so on the managed tiers the real cost driver is hosted compute — running your fits and the sandboxed agent on infrastructure we operate. Honest footnotes (this is the whole pitch)

  • Dollar prices are proposed / illustrative. The $50 / $200 per-seat figures and the "$0 Free" are a starting sketch; the founder sets the finals. The enforceable contract is the limits and feature gates in auth/plans.py, which this page mirrors exactly.
  • Self-hosting is free and unlimited. The OSS core under Apache 2.0 has no seat, project, or fit limits when you run it yourself with auth off. The billed Free plan is the managed-platform entry point, a different thing.
  • SOC 2: readiness, not certified. The control set is built toward SOC 2-style expectations (access control, audit, isolation), but the platform is not SOC 2 certified and no audit has been performed — see the Trust & Security page. Treat it as readiness evidence, not an attestation.
