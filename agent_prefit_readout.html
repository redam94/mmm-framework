It records every structural assumption, all 33 prior distributions, what those priors imply before seeing the data's verdict, and the checks the specification passed — so the final fitted model can be judged against choices made in advance, not after the fact.

Why pre-register a model design

  • It separates choices from findings — assumptions and priors are on record before the data could tempt anyone to bend them.
  • It makes the final model auditable — every change between this document and the fitted model must be justified in the change record.
  • It catches broken designs early — prior predictive and calibration checks fail cheaply here, not expensively after the fit.

The response shapes the priors imply

Before any data, the priors already commit each channel to a concave response and a decaying carryover. The bands below show how loosely: a wide band is a prior holding the question open, a narrow band is a commitment that should be defensible on subject-matter grounds.

Prior predictive checks

Could this model have produced our data?

Across 500 datasets simulated purely from the priors, the 90% prior-predictive band covers 30% of the observed series. Only 0.0% of simulated outcomes are negative. A healthy prior wraps the observed data loosely — neither excluding it nor allowing absurd magnitudes.

Next steps

Between this document and the fit

With the design recorded, the next step is the fit itself, followed by convergence diagnostics, posterior-predictive checks and the Media Performance Readout. Any change to priors or structure after seeing results should come back through this document first.

Design→Fit→Diagnose→Prioritize→Experiment→Calibrate
  • AnalyticsFit the model exactly as specified here; any deviation goes through the change record first.
  • AnalyticsCheck convergence (R-hat, ESS, divergences) and posterior-predictive fit before reading any effect.
  • TogetherJudge the fitted model in the Media Performance Readout against the commitments made in this document.
